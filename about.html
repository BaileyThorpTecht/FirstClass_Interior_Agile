The Development Team

We comprise the Development Team, and we have been hard at work creating this website for Smith Limited. Here you can find all the best tech products you could need. Heres the people behind it

Project Manager

Anjolina Nadan

Anjolina is the project manager, and she is responsible for supervising and organising the entire project. She oversees all the planning, scheduling, resource allocation, and budget management. The project manager makes sure that the project stays on track, meets the standards and is delivered to the stakeholder within the allocated time and is met to the standards. They also keep in touch with the stakeholder to provide regular updates of the project.

Designer

Carol Do

Carol is the project designer, she is responsible for designing the visual aspects and making it easier for the stakeholder to navigate the application. She works with the stakeholder to understand how they can get the best experience out of our product and to make the stakeholders request about the project incorporated, such as making it more visually appealing. Carol focuses on creating a seamless user experience, including the layout, navigation, and overall look and feel of the product. She does this by creating wireframes of the design as a prototype to show the design before the development phase.

Developer

Bailey Thorp

Bailey is the project developer, he is responsible for writing the code and building software based on the design that the designer has made. He turns the design into function coding components, and makes sure that the software meets the requirements. Bailey also works to maintain code quality and sticks to the coding standards, he is also responsible for unit testing to identify and fix and bugs in the programming.

Software Tester

Arthur Moore

Arthur is the project tester, the testers play a very important role in ensuring the quality of the software. He creates test plans, test cases, and test scripts based on the project and designs requirements. The testers goal is to identify and report defects or issues in the software, making sure that it meets the specified quality standards before it is deployed or released.
